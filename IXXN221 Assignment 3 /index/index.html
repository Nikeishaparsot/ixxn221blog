Week Two 18.07

The beginning of the lecture was particularly interesting as we dove into the history behind web design and what it was like when it first appeared in the world. I found it interesting that the name of Google was deliberately a misspelling of the word googol meaning 10 to the power of 100. The introduction of Assignment 1 was about user personas which I thought was an interesting concept as a project especially because I wasn’t sure where it would lead in the next assignments. But as I started to develop my user persona I realised how important it is to empathise with users when designing for a particular audience. It is most definitely a crucial process that allows us to be user-centred in the design approach.

Week Three 25.07

This week’s lecture included some history of web design from 1998 to 2008 but particularly it was the British student, Alex Tew’s Million Dollar Home page launched in 2005 that really caught my eye during this lecture. I thought it was such a cool concept that he created a place where you could click on the brand's logo on this image and then it would take you to their business. Although visually it is overwhelming, it is something I've never seen or known existed. I also didn’t realise how important the aesthetics and user experience can be in the online space but given that 79% of business-to-customer users say that the user experience is as important as the company products/services, it is a significant contributing factor to the success of a website.

Week Four 01.08

today's lecture was about the most recent development in web design starting from 2009 and progressing to the present day. One thing I learned was that Adobe XD existed which I am now very intrigued to try. We also covered customer journey maps and user flows and how they are used to understand the user. When I was creating my customer journey map for my user persona Isabella, it made me feel more like I was her and what I would want my own experience to be. It made me visualise the entire experience but putting it on a pdf. It was easier to identify what most people probably experience when they interact with websites and what their pain points are throughout their experience. This was the same with creating the user flow. As I’ve been a user myself on an eCommerce website, I’ve had my own problems interacting with poorly designed sites. It was easier to see where confusion can appear in a user interaction when I started the user flow but it also was easier to see what could make a website more intuitive for a visitor on the website.

Week Six 15.08

As we were introduced to assignment 2.1, we looked at colour within web design. The lecture talked about colour which I actually found more intriguing than I thought it would be because I didn’t realise what the different variants were used for (RGB, CMYK, Pantone). When I went to create a colour palette on coolors, I found a palette that I wanted to use, but having just started a style guide or the assignment, I found it was too difficult to use multtiple colours alongside black and white. Also how accessibility plays a part in web design. When I would place coloured text over another colour in my palette, in my mind I would think that it looked good and that it was easy to read because I could, but in reality, it failed the WebAim contrast checker. This was another reason I found using a colour palette with more colours difficult as opposed to sticking to one primary colour and then the black and white text. However, for my final design, I would like to have a minimalistic design and use as few colours as possible I think will be more successful in doing so.

Week Seven 05.09

Learning about mental models I thought was a useful diagram to look at for this course because it means you can create interfaces that truly align with a user’s expectations. By understanding how users perceive and interpret information, as a designer it means the interfaces become more intuitive and easier to use. But at the same time when designing, your website becomes intuitive to you as you’re designing it but probably not to all users. Week Nine 19.09

The responsiveness of a website is something I have never really considered until this course. It wasn’t something I paid much attention to. However, actually building a website and seeing how bad and unappealing it can be when it's not thought out properly definitely makes me more aware of good design in websites. There is no doubt that it would improve user engagement when switching from a computer to a phone or vice versa. I have definitely encountered a website or two where their responsiveness is inadequate and found it difficult to see particular elements. For a user experience, it is frustrating and would result in the loss of a potential customer for the business in question.
